Dementia Home Protection

From time to time, family members and caregivers must leave a person with cognitive impairment alone at home. An important step in protecting your home from dementia is our medical alarm system.

Alzheimer's

Leaving a loved one with Alzheimer's disease or another type of cognitive impairment can be especially stressful. Although a nursing home may be the best option, it is also possible to keep them safe at home and prevent them from walking at a reasonable cost. Although they can monitor and allow someone freedom during the early and middle stages of the disease.

Tech Solutions for Alzheimer's Care

Today's technological advances have made using our devices easy, although some may think that a person with Alzheimer's disease cannot use them. This means that using a watch or collar with fall detection and GPS geofencing can be just as simple for families who have a loved one with a disability and cognitive impairment who may be prone to falling and wandering.

Fall Detection for Alzheimer's

Because falls are the most common cause of injuries among adults over age 65 and because falls are even more likely in people with Alzheimer's disease, fall detection is a crucial component of our alert systems.

Essential Mobile Wander Alerts

Mobile wander alerts and geofencing are essential because more than half of Alzheimer's patients move, forget where they are going, become disoriented, and get lost. If your loved one is lost outside due to their condition, they may not be able to call for help. They can request help with GPS (global positioning system) monitoring with a single button.

Medical Alert for Alzheimer's Safety

It is often helpful to use our device that has a call button to call for help when a person has Alzheimer's disease and is still partially able to function. It is best to make this decision with caution because it is based on observation of your loved one. In the middle stages of cognitive decline, fall detection becomes increasingly crucial because your loved one is likely to be less coherent. Considering the progression of the disease, consider our medical alert service as additional security.
